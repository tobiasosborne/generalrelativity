The Lie bracket as a parallelogram defect

Walk ε along v then w — vs w then v. The gap closes to ε2·[v,w] as ε → 0.

v then w (endpoint A)    w then v (endpoint B)    gap A − B

Step size

0.40

Field pair

Two paths, one starting point. Pick a base point p (drag it on the canvas) and a step size ε. The green path walks ε along v, then ε along w; the blue path walks ε along w, then ε along v. In a flat space with v, w commuting, both paths land at the same point. In general the endpoints differ by

A − B = ε2 [v, w]|p + O(ε3),

with the Lie bracket [v,w]j = vi ∂iwj − wi ∂ivj. Drag the ε slider down: the gap shrinks like ε2. Now turn on Rescale by 1/ε2: the rescaled gap arrow stops shrinking and locks onto the analytic [v,w] vector — the bracket emerges as the ε → 0 limit of the parallelogram defect.
