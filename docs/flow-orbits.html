Starting points

What you're seeing: a single vector field v on the chart with components Vu = 0.85 + 0.15·v, Vv = 0.55·cos(0.7·u). Click anywhere to set the starting point p; the green curve is the orbit Cp(t) = Φt(p) obtained by solving du/dt = Vu, dv/dt = Vv. The gold dot is Φt(p) at the current time; the gold arrow at p is the tangent vector v|p — literally the field value at p, and equivalently dCp/dt at t=0.

Different starting points give different orbits, but they all share the same field: that is what Φs ∘ Φt = Φt+s says — flowing for time t then for time s is the same as flowing for time t+s, regardless of where you started.
